## Coworking Space Reviews: Medellín & Florianópolis

---

### Nómada Hub — Medellín, Colombia

Tucked into the El Poblado neighborhood near Parque Lleras, **Nómada Hub** charges around **COP 420,000/month** for hot-desk access. Gigabit fiber via Claro Colombia delivers rock-solid speeds, rarely dipping below 300 Mbps. Amenities include a rooftop terrace, standing desks, a cold-brew station, and two podcast-ready soundbooths. The vibe skews creative-professional — expect product designers and indie founders. Evening networking events organized by co-manager **Valentina Ríos** keep the community unusually tight-knit.

---

### Selva Cowork — Medellín, Colombia

Located in Laureles on Avenida 33, **Selva Cowork** runs **COP 380,000/month** for hot desks. Internet is dual-ISP redundant (ETB + UNE), averaging 200 Mbps symmetrical. Botanical decor, a proper espresso bar, phone booths, and a courtyard hammock zone define the space. The atmosphere is relaxed yet focused — popular with remote employees from international tech firms. Front-desk manager **Diego Carmona** is reliably helpful with SIM cards and local logistics.

---

### Praia Desk — Florianópolis, Brazil

Steps from Lagoa da Conceição, **Praia Desk** charges roughly **BRL 650/month** for a hot desk. Vivo Fibra powers the 500 Mbps connection with consistent uptime. Highlights include surf-rack storage, a juice bar stocked with açaí, private call rooms, and a rooftop with lagoon views. The crowd blends Brazilian digital nomads with international surfers working startup hours. Community manager **Isabela Fontana** runs weekly wellness workshops that draw strong attendance.

---

### Canto Sul Workspace — Florianópolis, Brazil

Situated in the Itacorubi tech corridor near **ACATE Innovation Hub**, **Canto Sul** prices hot desks at **BRL 720/month**. A dedicated 1 Gbps fiber line handles video-heavy workloads effortlessly. The space features a maker lab with 3D printers, a curated business library, ergonomic Steelcase seating, and an outdoor deck. Ideal for hardware entrepreneurs and deep-focus workers. Events coordinator **Rafael Menezes** organizes monthly pitch nights that frequently attract local VC scouts from Florianópolis's growing startup ecosystem.
