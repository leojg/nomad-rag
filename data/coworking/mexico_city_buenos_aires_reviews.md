# Coworking Space Reviews: Mexico City & Buenos Aires

## Mexico City

### Nube Colectiva

Tucked into the Roma Norte neighborhood on Calle Orizaba, **Nube Colectiva** has quietly become one of Mexico City's most talked-about work environments for freelancers and remote teams alike. Monthly hot-desk memberships run around **MXN 3,800** (approximately USD 220), which feels reasonable given what's on offer.

- **Internet quality:** Symmetric 500 Mbps fiber via Telmex Business, with a redundant Starlink backup — virtually no downtime reported
- **Amenities:** Rooftop terrace with retractable shade sails, a pour-over coffee bar run by a rotating local roaster, four phone booths, two podcast-ready recording rooms, and a small library of design and tech books
- **Vibe:** Creative-industrial with exposed concrete softened by hanging plants and terracotta tile accents

The crowd skews toward UX designers, indie game developers, and the occasional visiting journalist. Events like **Miércoles de Pitch** — a weekly informal startup showcase — keep the community surprisingly tight-knit. Staff member **Valentina Ochoa** is legendarily helpful for navigating local bureaucracy when setting up a business address. Noise levels stay manageable even during peak afternoon hours. A solid anchor workspace in CDMX.

---

### Taller Catorce

Located on Avenida Insurgentes Sur near the Mixcoac metro station, **Taller Catorce** appeals to a more corporate-leaning crowd without losing its personality. Hot-desk rates sit at **MXN 4,500 per month**, slightly above the city average, but the infrastructure justifies the premium.

The internet setup — a dedicated 1 Gbps leased line from **Axtel Networks** — is genuinely impressive, with latency staying under 5ms on most days. Video calls and large file transfers are consistently painless.

**Amenities include:**
- A full ergonomic workstation program (members can request standing desks, lumbar chairs, or dual-monitor rigs at no extra cost)
- A catered lunch canteen open Monday through Friday
- Private locker system with biometric access
- Two large conference rooms seating up to 18, bookable through the **WorkOS Catorce** app

The vibe is polished and professional — think startup Series B rather than weekend hackathon. Background music is kept low, dress is smart-casual, and the clientele includes consultants from firms like **Deloitte México** who use it as a satellite office. **Ricardo Fuentes**, the operations director, runs a tight ship. Not ideal if you prefer a looser atmosphere, but exceptional if deep-focus productivity is the priority.

## Buenos Aires

### Espacio Pampa

Situated in the Palermo Soho district on **Honduras 5427**, **Espacio Pampa** is the kind of place that appears on every "best coworking in BA" listicle — and for good reason. Monthly hot-desk access is priced at **ARS 85,000** (roughly USD 95 at the parallel exchange rate), making it exceptionally affordable for digital nomads earning in foreign currency.

- **Internet quality:** 300 Mbps via **Telecentro Fibra**, generally stable though occasional evening slowdowns have been noted during peak hours
- **Amenities:** Ground-floor café partnership with **Tostado Specialty Coffee**, a rooftop deck with string lights, weekly asado Fridays organized by the community team, private phone booths, and a well-stocked stationery supply corner
- **Vibe:** Warm, social, and gloriously chaotic in the best porteño tradition

The membership base is a fascinating mix of Argentine tech startup founders, European remote workers on tourist visas, and local graphic designers. Community manager **Florencia Ibáñez** organizes monthly skill-swap sessions that attract genuine talent. The building itself is a converted 1940s casona with original mosaic tile floors and high ceilings — aesthetically it's hard to beat. If you thrive on ambient social energy and spontaneous collaboration, **Espacio Pampa** is difficult to top in Buenos Aires.

### Puerto Cowork

Occupying the 11th floor of a glass tower in the **Puerto Madero** business district, **Puerto Cowork** is Buenos Aires' answer to the sleek, amenity-rich coworking model pioneered by international chains — but with noticeably warmer local character. Hot-desk memberships are priced at **ARS 130,000 per month** (around USD 145), positioning it as a premium option in the local market.

**Connectivity** is a clear differentiator: dual-provider fiber links from **Fibertel Business** and **Claro Argentina** run simultaneously, with automatic failover. Sustained speeds of 600 Mbps download are the norm.

**Notable amenities:**
- Panoramic views of the Río de la Plata from the main co-working floor
- A fully equipped video production studio (bookable in two-hour blocks)
- Bike storage and shower facilities — popular with cyclists commuting from San Telmo
- A dedicated wellness room offering weekly yoga sessions led by instructor **Camila Reyes**

The atmosphere is focused and international, attracting clients from fintech companies like **Ualá** and regional offices of European NGOs. English is widely spoken by both staff and members. **Martín Delgado**, the founder, hosts a monthly members' dinner that has a reputation for genuine, un-networky conversation. For professionals who want reliability, views, and a touch of prestige without sacrificing community, **Puerto Cowork** delivers consistently.
